Quasars, High-Redshift Universe and Gravitational Lensing

I am broadly interested in many subfields in extragalactic astronomy and cosmology, with a focus on high-redshift quasars and strong gravitational lensing. I am currently trying to solve the following open questions:

  • How do quasars form? How do they impact their host galaxies and their environments, especially at high redshifts?
  • How can we utilize gravitational lensing to probe faint distant objects?
  • How can we find more quasars and other interesting objects with upcoming facilities like LSST?

A survey of high-redshift gravitationally lensed quasars. I am leading a survey for lensed quasars at z>~5, which is the first systematic survey for such objects. I made a mock catalog of lensed quasars to analyze the statistics and observational features of these objects (check it here!). The Figure above shows a few of our discoveries.

High-resolution imaging of z~7 quasar host galaxies. As a Co-I of a few JWST programs targeting at z>~7 quasars, I will analyze the high-resolution images of these quasars and measure their host galaxy properties. For many of these quasars, we will also get their high-resolution ALMA image and explore the sub-kpc structures in their host galaxies.
