Quasars, High-Redshift Universe and Gravitational Lensing

I am broadly interested in many subfields in extragalactic astronomy and cosmology, with a focus on high-redshift quasars and strong gravitational lensing. I am currently trying to solve the following open questions:

  • How do quasars form? How do they impact their host galaxies and their environments, especially at high redshifts?

  • How can we utilize gravitational lensing to probe faint distant objects and to constrain cosmological parameters?

  • How can we find more quasars and other interesting objects with upcoming facilities like LSST?

As an observer, I use both large survey datasets and multi-wavelength observations to investigate the questions above.

My PhD thesis is a survey for lensed quasars at z>~5, which is the first systematic survey for such objects. To develop a better candidate selection algorithm, I made a mock catalog with observations of lensed quasars to analyze the observational features of these objects (check it here!).
